Machine learning is a branch of artificial intelligence that focuses on building systems capable of learning from data and improving their performance over time without being explicitly programmed. It enables computers to identify patterns, make predictions, and take decisions based on experience. Machine learning algorithms are widely used in applications such as recommendation systems, image recognition, speech processing, fraud detection, and autonomous vehicles.

There are three major types of machine learning: supervised learning, unsupervised learning, and reinforcement learning. In supervised learning, models are trained using labeled data, while unsupervised learning works with unlabeled data to discover hidden structures. Reinforcement learning focuses on learning optimal actions through trial and error interactions with an environment.

As data continues to grow rapidly, machine learning plays a crucial role in modern technology by enabling smarter systems and more efficient solutions across industries such as healthcare, finance, education, and entertainment.
